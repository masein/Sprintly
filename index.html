Everything a small team needs

Boards, sprints, and the boring-but-vital stuff.

Shipped and stable in v1.0.0 — the whole roadmap, from hardening to polish.

Boards & swimlanes

Kanban with To do · In progress · Done out of the box. Group into swimlanes, save filtered views, add your own columns.

Sprints & burndown

A named window with a goal. Plan it, start it, watch the burndown, then complete it straight into a retro.

Backlog triage

The pile. Multi-select and triage in one go — assign, label, move to a sprint, or clear it out.

Git integration ⭐

GitHub · GitLab · Gitea. Link branches, commits, and PRs to tasks; auto-transition on merge; push commit status back.

CI/CD status

Pass / fail / pending chips on linked PRs and cards — always icon + label, never colour alone.

Subtasks, labels & fields

Break work down, tag it with coloured labels, and add the custom fields — text, number, select, date — your team actually uses.

Flow metrics

Cycle time, lead time, throughput, and a cumulative-flow diagram — computed from real status transitions.

Notifications & @mentions

Pinged for mentions, assignments, and the tasks you watch — live over WebSocket, and never for your own actions.

Webhooks, Slack & Discord

Signed, retried deliveries with first-class Slack and Discord targets — and a per-project admin UI to test them.

Roadmap & timeline

Epics as bars, milestones as markers over a date axis. Progress is done over total — no vanity bars.

Time tracking & invoicing

Track time on tasks, then turn billable hours and rates into per-client invoices as PDF or CSV.

Templates & recurrence

Skeleton tasks that prefill, and recurring templates the worker materialises daily, weekly, or monthly.

Self-hosted by default

Your server. Your data. No watching.

One docker compose up brings the whole stack online. There's no telemetry phoning home, and no manager dashboard surveilling personal signals — that's a design rule, not a setting.

  • Reversible migrations and scheduled backups with a guarded, audit-logged restore.
  • Import from Trello or CSV; export a full project to JSON or CSV. Your data is never hostage.
  • Public read-only status pages, an installable PWA, and a board that works down to 375px.
  • Accessibility baked in: colour is never the only cue.
